Agridera Fresh Market Cabbage Hybrids

Agridera aims to be one of the world's leading companies in the breeding, production, and marketing of cabbage seeds. We invest intensively in research and development to provide solutions to growers and consumers in numerous countries around the world. In particular, we breed improved disease-resistant cultivars adapted to different markets globally.


Currently, emphasis is placed on combining Cabbage Yellow Leaf Curl Virus (CYLCV) and Cabbage Spotted Wilt Virus (CSWV) resistances with other soil borne diseases into superior cabbage breeding lines and hybrids. Moreover, comprehensive efforts are being undertaken to develop varieties resistance to CBRFV virus (Cabbage Brown Rugose Fruit Virus).


In terms of horticultural traits, breeding is for earliness, different head sizes, firmness, green color, flavor, crack resistance, shelf life, and high/low temperature growth. Combinations of quality features and disease resistance are sought in a variety of types, namely round and elongated shapes.

Agirdera Cabbage breeding programs are focusing on three main segments: Greenhouse Indeterminate Cabbages, Open-field Indeterminate Cabbages, and Bush Type Open-field Cabbages.

Greenhouse Cabbage Breeding Program

This breeding is aimed for the Spanish and Turkish markets. For single and cluster picks, round head shape and resistances of CYLCV, CSWV, Nematodes and FORL (Fusarium oxysporum f.sp. radicis-brassicae). Some hybrids may include more resistances, such as Cladosporium and Leveillula Taurica (Lv).

Open-field Indeterminate Cabbages

This breeding is for Brazilian, Moroccan, and Turkish markets. The main focus is on large round shape heads (200 grams plus); and resistances of CYLCV, CSWV, Nematodes and FORL. In the near future, F3 (Fusarium oxysporum f.sp. brassicae) will be included as basic resistance in our cultivars.

Open-field Bush Type Cabbages

This breeding is for Mediterranean markets. We breed round and elongated shapes, standard sizes (140- 180 grams), and large sizes (min. 180 grams up to 300 grams and more). We combine the resistances of CYLCV, CSWV and Nematodes as basic resistances in our hybrids. However, some hybrids may include more resistances, such as Cladosporium and Leveillula Taurica (Lv).

Agridera operates through international and local distributers around the world.
